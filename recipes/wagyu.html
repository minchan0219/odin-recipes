Wagyu Beef

DESCRIPTION:

Wagyu beef is a Japanese beef cattle breed that is known for its high marbling, which gives it a rich, buttery flavor and tender texture. Wagyu beef is typically more expensive than other types of beef, but it is considered to be a delicacy by many people. The high marbling of Wagyu beef is due to a number of factors, including the breed of cattle, the diet of the cattle, and the way the cattle are raised. Wagyu beef is typically graded on a scale of 1 to 5, with 5 being the highest grade. Wagyu beef that is graded 5 is considered to be the highest quality and is the most expensive.

INGREDIENTS:

  • Wagyu beef (any cut will do, but ribeye, New York strip, or filet mignon are all good choices)
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • Vegetable oil (or olive oil)
  • Garlic (minced or sliced)
  • Butter (salted or unsalted)
  • Parsley (chopped)

STEPS:

  1. Preheat a large skillet over medium-high heat.
  2. Add the vegetable oil to the skillet and heat until shimmering.
  3. Season the steak with salt and pepper.
  4. Place the steak in the skillet and cook for 2-3 minutes per side, or until desired doneness.
  5. Remove the steak from the skillet and let it rest for 5 minutes.
  6. While the steak is resting, melt the butter in the skillet.
  7. Add the garlic to the skillet and cook for 1 minute, or until fragrant.
  8. Add the parsley to the skillet and stir to combine.
  9. Slice the steak and serve with the garlic-parsley butter sauce.
